Methodology

Our project aims to create a tool to help people better understand the landscape of social media from four different scales to compare the strengths and weaknesses of social media. To complete this goal, we identified four key objectives:

  • 1. Social Media Analysis
  • 2. Behavior and Wellness Survey
  • 3. Web Design
  • 4. User Study

Analyze social media with respect to platform design and profit.
Our first approach to analyze social media sites was to examine the design features of each platform. We also examined each platform's revenue generation and advertising strategies, and identified similarities and differences between platforms.
To gain an overall view of a social media, it is important to understand the scale of its user base, as well as how an owner makes a profit off of their users. In order to examine each social media, we collected the following data:

  • Annual Revenue: to provide insight into the financial success of the platform.
  • Parent Company and/or Owner, to provide context on the management of the platform.
  • Monthly Users, to quantify the user base and popularity of the platform.

We narrowed down key questions about design to determine what was available or missing from various platforms. Additionally, we collected all relevant design features per social media, and condensed the list into a ranked top-three list.

The following questions were used to analyze revenue and funding methods:

  • Are there advertisements? Are these advertisements recommended?
  • What is the advertising revenue (if public)?
  • Is user data being sold? To whom?
  • Does it accept donations?
  • Is it a publicly traded company?

The data was collected through secondary research and analyzed using qualitative and quantitative methods to identify patterns and trends. The findings were used to compare and contrast the different social media platforms, highlighting their strengths and weaknesses.

Personal Experience and Internal Bias
Our personal experience with social media will affect how we perceive and analyze this subject. As both of us are college students, the time period in which we were raised will give us a better understanding of some social media over others.
Following is a quick estimate of our personal experience with each. “Extensive user” means visiting the platform daily or more than daily, while “intermittent user” means visiting the platform approximately once a week or once a month. These dates are not precise, but should serve as a helpful guide for potential bias for future analysis of our findings.

  1. BeReal: Extensive users from June 2022 to September, one author continues to use it daily.
  2. Facebook: Non-users. Profiles created, but rarely if ever populated.
  3. Instagram: Extensive users pre-2020, but current intermittent users.
  4. LinkedIn:Intermittent users since 2019.
  5. Reddit: One author was an extensive user from 2018 to 2021. The other author is an intermittent user since 2019.
  6. Snapchat: Extensive users since 2015.
  7. Tik Tok: Extensive users in 2020, intermittent users from 2021 to 2022.
  8. Tumblr: Extensive users pre-2018, one author was an extensive user in 2022, the other was intermittent in 2022.
  9. Twitch: One author was an intermittent user in 2020-2021, while the other was an extensive user in 2020-2021.
  10. Twitter: One author was an extensive user from 2015 to 2018 and 2020 to 2022, the other was an intermittent user in 2019 to 2022.
  11. YouTube: Extensive users since 2011 and 2014.
  12. 4Chan: Non-users.
