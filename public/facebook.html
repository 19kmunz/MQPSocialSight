Design Overview

Facebook features an infinitely scrollable feed of photos, videos, and texts, both from people you know and suggested by Facebook. On Facebook you can build and maintain a profile to present yourself - your photos, thoughts, ideas and activities. You can connect with people you may know or groups of strangers by sharing or re-sharing posts, publicly commenting, or private messaging. Your Home feed includes suggested content, while the Feeds tab allows you to curate a filtered list of content you want to see, without suggestions.

Infinite Combined Feed

Your feed is infinite, meaning that there will never be a second page. The feed shows you posts by people you follow, as well as content recommended by an algorithm.

Curated Friends List

Maintain a list of people you want to connect with by sending and receiving friend requests, and adding your contacts. You can share posts, direct message, and post memories.

Post and Re-Share

Post text, image, or video content or repost content from other pages. You can post to all of your friends, specific people, or community-driven groups.


Analysis

Below is our analysis of Facebook based on survey data gathered by participants.

This section seeks to compare Facebook's ability to build and support communities. In general, users agree that Facebook encourages positive communities, although negatvity is still common. Click the dropdown to see specifics.

People frequently see positive conversation on Facebook. This is because communities are built around family, friends, and people in groups with mutual interests.

Frequently see negative conversation on Facebook. Extended families may argue, or some communities or users may debate or troll to upset others. Less personal relationships cause more negativity.

People occasionally see views that differ from theirs, which may lead to debate

Facebook remains a popular platform for connecting with loved ones, however, the platform frequently displays newsfeed and recommended content. Although users still primarily see posts from their friends, there is a tight balance between these posts and the suggested content, while other social media platforms, like BeReal and Instagram, place a greater emphasis on personal connections.

Users strongly agree that Facebook prioritizes sponsored posts, meaning that corporate interest may be prioritized over user posts. Instagram follows in agreement, and Meta Inc. is well known for profiting off of advertisements.

Most people agree that two-way discussion is common on Facebook, and people overwhelmingly see discussion frequently. This may be because people often respond to posts from friends and family on their feed.

In general, people agree that it is easy to make new friends on Facebook. There are a considerable number of people that disagree, and this may depend on your involvement in groups or other pages.

This section seeks to understand whether individual identities are encouraged by Facebook, which users generally agree on. Click the dropdown to see specifics.

People agree that they interact with other individuals on Facebook. Often these are people that users know, or friends of their friends, so this close connection helps users feel more real.

People tend to agree. This social media is more personal, revolving around the traditional profile and feed of friends' posts.

People agree that their Facebook personas are authentic. This is how they present themselves to family and friends, so it will relate to themselves.

Users agree strongly that Facebook enables profile customization. This includes headers and profile pictures, however all posts also appear on the user's profile.

People slightly disagree that they spend time curating their profiles, which means they likely do not edit it after creation.

This section targets the value of content on Facebook, including entertainment and educational value, as well as ease to create new content. Click the dropdown to see specifics.

Most people say that they see posts by small creators on social media occasionally, with many seeing this content frequently.

Most people use Facebook for less than 30 minutes, and many use it for less than 10. People may be making a quick update, responding to messages, or viewing a few posts.

Many people feel that Facebook prioritizes image content, although answers vary, meaning that Facebook may support a variety of content.

People agree that content is easy to create, since social media is designed to encourage posting.

People follow the overall trend of being mostly entertained by social media, with most people responding that they are frequently or occasionally entertained. These are follower-based and have weaker recommendation algorithms.

Many users say they are occasionally educated by Facebook, but most say they are rarely or never educated. This may be due to the personal style of many posts, as well as misinformation.

Surprisingly, Facebook is one of the platforms that struggles the most compared to other media we asked about. This could be a variety of reasons, such as not enjoying your connections posts or the recommended content, or that our respondents for facebook skewed older and that generation reflects on their view experience differently. Whatever the reason, keep this in mind!

Facebook's Cambridge Analytica scandal as forever marked it as susceptible to misinformation, especially through it's targeted ads. Posts are linked to how often they've been shared (likes, re-shared,etc.), so inflammatory misinformation is a tantalizing route to internet fame. The echo-chambers of community bubbles or recommendation bubbles can also contribute to a space where misinformation is not questioned.

Here, we seek to understand how much user control Facebook encourages. This includes moderation and control of advertisements. While responses slightly agree that Facebook allows for user control, answers vary. Click the dropdown to see specifics.

The recommendation algorithm can feel out of a user's control. Although friendships are valuable on the platform, the lean towards newsfeed and other recommendation algorithms may make the user feel powerless at times.

Facebook makes $114 billion dollars annually off of its ad spots. Although there are some tools for hiding certain ads and understanding what demographics users are targeted as, they may not be widely known or understood by users.

Although Facebook's recommendation algorithm will recommend you your interests, it is not as good at it as its video counterparts. Your connections' posts will show up frequently, which may or may not relate to your hobbies.

The recommendation algorithm is not very strong, but can still recommend content that feels like a waste of time or makes your day a little worse. If you can stick to content from trusted loved ones, you may avoid some of this content, but Facebook was one of the lower performing platforms on this question.

Facebook has struggled with its content moderation. Reportedly, Facebook used to prioritized posts with "angry" reactions in the news feed, which was criticized for likely spreading more controversial content that could lead to more controversy within user spaces. Facebook struggles with scammers and data harvesting with third party apps, which can lead to, in the worst cases, identity theft.

Many users revisit content from Facebook, whether to share funny videos with friends or to look back on photos with loved ones. The "saved" and "memories" pages let you look back on content you've made or marked.
